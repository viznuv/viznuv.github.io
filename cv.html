Background

I am an Assistant Professor of Marketing at Jio Institute, with research interests at the intersection of artificial intelligence, consumer behaviour, and social networks. My work investigates how emerging technologies — including AI, blockchain, and metaverse platforms — reshape consumer decision-making, brand–consumer relationships, and societal dynamics. I employ rigorous experimental and computational methodologies, integrating behavioural theory with data-driven approaches to advance both marketing scholarship and practice. My research programme is organised around three interconnected pillars: (1) Consumer Psychology in Virtual Environments; (2) Algorithmic Marketing & Human–AI Interaction; and (3) Brand Ethics & Societal Engagement. My work has been recognised with the Franco Nicosia Best Paper Award (ACR, 2025), the Bala & Vasantha Balachandran Faculty Research Grant (NASMEI, 2025), and the Sheth Foundation Marketing Research Grant (AIM–AIB, 2025). I have also been selected as a Raisina Young Fellow by the Observer Research Foundation (2026). I spearheaded content and planning for Jio's AI Classroom (600,000+ participants). Prior to academia, I served as a Product Manager at Vodafone India.

Research Programme

Pillar I — Consumer Psychology in Virtual Environments

Focus: Psychological mechanisms (FOMO, scarcity, social influence) in novel consumption contexts.

  • NFT Purchase Behaviour — psychological antecedents of NFT decisions [JCB, 2023]
  • Streaming Platform Dynamics — survey × behavioural-trace triangulation [R&R, ABDC A*]
  • Metaverse Service Perception — warmth × competence frameworks [Under Review R1, ABDC A]
  • Social-Commerce Overconsumption — multi-method pathways and self-regulation [Under Review, ABDC A]

Pillar II — Algorithmic Marketing & Human–AI Interaction

Focus: How algorithms shape and are shaped by consumer behaviour.

  • AI Recommendation Systems — multilevel mixed-effects and causal estimators
  • Generative AI for Personalisation — real-time fine-tuning workflows
  • Human–LLM Opinion Dynamics — extended dialogues, lexical entropy and belief distance
  • Brand Representations in LLMs — benchmarks for Generative Engine Optimization [NASMEI Grant, 2025]
  • Influencer Marketing Signal Interference — authenticity and fit moderating disclosure [Under Review, ABDC A*]

Pillar III — Brand Ethics & Societal Engagement

Focus: Normative dimensions of brand–consumer relationships.

  • Brand Activism & Trust — congruence and message believability [Franco Nicosia, ACR 2025]
  • Expectation Violation & Recovery — lab, field, and event studies of activism missteps
  • Cause-Related Marketing — values, emotions, product type [In Revision, ABDC A]
  • Sustainable Consumption — norms, emotions, motives in recommerce [In Revision, ABDC A]
