Research Programme

Three Interconnected Pillars

A coherent agenda bridging behavioural theory and computational rigour — examining how virtual environments, algorithmic systems, and brand ethics intersect to shape consumer decision-making and society.

I

Consumer Psychology in Virtual Environments

Focus: Psychological mechanisms — FOMO, scarcity, social influence — operating in novel digital consumption contexts.

  • NFT Purchase Behaviour: Investigated psychological antecedents of NFT decisions, examining FOMO, reinforcement sensitivity, and perceived scarcity as key drivers. [Journal of Consumer Behaviour, 2023 · ABDC A]
  • Streaming Platform Dynamics: Survey × behavioural-trace triangulation examining platform cues, password-sharing behaviours, and viewer engagement tactics. [R&R, ABDC A*]
  • Metaverse Service Perception: Warmth × competence frameworks driving engagement and word-of-mouth in immersive retail/service environments. [Under Review R1, ABDC A]
  • Social-Commerce Overconsumption: Multi-method examination of psychological pathways and self-regulation mechanisms. [Under Review, ABDC A]
See related publications →
II

Algorithmic Marketing & Human–AI Interaction

Focus: How algorithms shape — and are shaped by — consumer behaviour.

  • AI Recommendation Systems: Compared personalised, collaborative-filter, and generic engines using multilevel mixed-effects and causal estimators.
  • Generative AI for Personalisation: Real-time fine-tuning workflows aligning LLMs with shopper intent.
  • Human–LLM Opinion Dynamics: Extended dialogues reduce lexical entropy yet widen belief distance on contentious topics.
  • Brand Representations in LLMs: Developing benchmarks for Generative Engine Optimization. [NASMEI Grant, 2025]
  • Influencer Marketing Signal Interference: How authenticity and fit moderate disclosure effects. [Under Review, ABDC A*]
See related publications →
III

Brand Ethics & Societal Engagement

Focus: Normative dimensions of brand–consumer relationships.

  • Brand Activism & Trust: Brand–cause–consumer balance framework examining how congruence and message believability strengthen trust while misalignment triggers reactance. [Franco Nicosia Award, ACR 2025]
  • Expectation Violation & Recovery: Lab, field, and event studies examining how timing, valence, and attribution of activism missteps unravel brand trust.
  • Cause-Related Marketing: Multi-method study on perceived values, emotions, and product type in charity-linked products. [In Revision, ABDC A]
  • Sustainable Consumption: Norms, emotions, and motives shaping recommerce behaviour. [In Revision, ABDC A]
